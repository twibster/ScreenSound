ScreenSound — icon concepts

Four concept sketches at the intended final size (256 px), a mid-range taskbar size (48 px), and the system-tray size (16 px). The same four cards render again on a dark surface below so you can check contrast in both theme modes, and at the bottom there's a simulated tray strip for each concept.

Concept 1 — Dual-monitor transfer

Two monitors with an arrow arcing between them. Tells the whole story; busiest at 16px.

Concept 2 — Monitor emitting waves

Single display, three soundwaves. Simplest silhouette; survives tiny sizes best.

Concept 3 — Speaker branching

One source, two outputs. Routing-first; no screens in the glyph.

Concept 4 — Monitor contains speaker

Display outline with a speaker glyph inside. Strongest silhouette; reads cleanly everywhere.

Concept 1 — on dark

Watch the grey outline — it can fade against dark chrome.

Monitor outline vs. dark bg.

Concept 3 — on dark

Filled speaker stays visible; watch the grey.

Concept 4 — on dark

Outline + inner glyph; check legibility at 16.

Concept 4 refinements

Three variants that try to push Concept 4 past "display with audio" toward "audio routed across monitors". 4a adds a depth cue (second monitor), 4b adds a direction cue (wave escapes the frame), 4c reframes the glyph as an app-plus-output badge. The original Concept 4 is repeated on the far left as a baseline.

4a — stacked monitors

4b — waves escape

4c — app window + speaker badge

Window inside the monitor; speaker as a corner badge. Per-app flavor.
